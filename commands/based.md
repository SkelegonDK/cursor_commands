# Evidence-Based Research Protocol

## Purpose
Before proposing solutions, I will ground my recommendations in real-world evidence by researching contemporary solutions from professionals in the relevant field, verifying information across multiple sources.

## Target Context
- **Domain:** Any task requiring factual, up-to-date, and professionally-validated solutions
- **Outcome:** Recommendations backed by verifiable evidence from authoritative sources

## Research Protocol

### Phase 1: Discovery
1. **Identify the domain** — What professional field(s) does this task fall under?
2. **Search for current solutions** — Find how professionals are solving this problem today
3. **Locate authoritative sources** — Official documentation, industry standards, expert publications

### Phase 2: Verification
4. **Cross-reference findings** — Compare information across at least 2-3 independent sources
5. **Check recency** — Prioritize recent solutions (within last 1-2 years when applicable)
6. **Assess credibility** — Favor official docs, peer-reviewed content, recognized experts

### Phase 3: Synthesis
7. **Identify consensus** — What approaches do multiple sources agree on?
8. **Note alternatives** — What legitimate competing approaches exist?
9. **Flag uncertainties** — Where do sources disagree or where is evidence thin?

## Source Hierarchy
When evaluating sources, prioritize in this order:
1. **Official documentation** — Primary source from creators/maintainers
2. **Industry standards** — RFC, ISO, professional body guidelines
3. **Peer-reviewed research** — Academic papers, technical reviews
4. **Expert practitioners** — Recognized professionals with verifiable credentials
5. **Community consensus** — Widely-adopted practices with track record

## Output Format
When presenting findings, I will:
- State the recommended approach clearly
- Cite specific sources that support it
- Note the date/version of information where relevant
- Acknowledge any limitations or alternatives
- Distinguish between established facts and emerging practices

## Constraints
- Reasoning: Search before suggesting; verify before concluding
- Scope: Focus research on the specific problem domain
- Self-check: Ensure recommendations are traceable to credible sources
- Transparency: Clearly indicate when information could not be verified
